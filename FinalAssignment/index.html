Example

The aim of our project is to display the distribution of different diseases all around the world. We therefore used datasets from the World Health Organization which delivers us the total number of cases for each country and every year for the diseases xx,yy,zz. We display this data on a map, using different colours in order to show which countries and parts of the world are extremely affected by the disease.

As a total number is not always extremely meaningful though, we wanted to recognize whether there is a relation between the wealth of the country and the number of diseases. Therefore, we colleted data about the Gross Domestic Product (GDP) of the countries. In order to display the relation, we divide the number of cases per country by the average GDP per person. High results are generally displayed in a darker red on the map.

Also, the users can choose to display the number of cases per inhabitant for every country as total numbers are not useful to compare big and small countries.

In order to show the trend over the years, there is a possibility to use a time picker to only show the data of specific years.
