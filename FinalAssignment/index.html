Diseases around the world

Final project - Interactive Data Exploration 2017/18

This web page aims at visualizing data from the WHO about the reported cases of different diseases. There are different visualizations being used on this site in order to demonstrate the distribution of the diseases for countries and regions and also in order to compare the diseases amount of incidences to the GDP per capita for each country.

Exploring Diphtheria distribution in different regions

These donut charts visualize the amount of incidences for each disease, split up by years and regions - according to the 6 WHO regions .

The left chart always displays the most recent data for the disease, while the year of the right chart can be changed.

  • Africa (AFR)
  • America (AMR)
  • Eastern Mediterranean (EMR)
  • Europe (EUR)
  • South-East Asia (SEAR)
  • Western Pacific (WPR)

There are 2 modes for the map. The Mode Total Cases colours the countries based only on the total number of cases per country.

The mode Population Affected takes into account the number of inhabitants for each country and colours the map according to the cases per inhabitant.

Diphtheria in India

This diagram shows the GDP per Capita (blue line) vs. the percentage of population affected (red line) for the chosen disease and country over 1999 - 2016.

The diagram is very useful to find out specific information about how the disease affects different economies differently.

For example, looking at Diphtheria in India and Madagascar, we can see that the cases are decreasing in India which is a fastly growing economy whereas in Madagascar, the cases are more.

Pertussis is an interesting case as even in high GDP countries such as in Denmark and growing economies like China, the disease seems to be growing but in low GDP countries such as Madagascar, it seems to be decreasing. This could be due to the fact that Pertussis can also occur in vaccinated people.

Conclusions

The exploration of the data about the 9 diseases, especially in compariso to the GDP of the countries was very exciting. Genereally speaking it can be said that some of the diseases seem to be very spread in Africa and South America. The map was a very good option to visualize this supposition. This fact is definitely not the case for all diseases though. For example, especially Mumps and Pertussis are diseases that are extremely spread in Europe and America. Therefore, the chosen visualizations on this website were a good choice to explore the used data.
Looking at the GDP vs. the cases for countries, some connections can be found for certain countries. Especially the disease Diphtheria seems to be more widespread in countries with a low GDP than in countries with a high GDP. While there is no clear corelation for all diseases's no of incidents with the GDP of the country, there are multiple factors that contribute to the increase in numbers such as density of the population since many viruses are spread through air, economy, vaccine available in the country etc
